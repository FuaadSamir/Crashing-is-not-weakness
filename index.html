A healthy and positive online life.

In the past decade, social media has moved from a place for communication and connecting with friends to an online hell for almost everyone using it. We've drifted far from the actual purpose of these platforms.

Studies report, in the last decade:

  • A 70% increase in anxiety and depression in teens and young adults.
  • Body image issues up by 62%, especially among girls aged 12-18.
  • Online harassment up by 65% across all age groups.
  • FOMO- Fear Of Missing Out, up by 60%.

    • Why should we be hopeful?

    The light at the end of this tunnel might be really dim, or might not even be visible to a lot of us. Yet its human nature to stay positive and keep trying.

    • Apps built around a sustainable and positive social media experience.
      1. BeReal- An app that encourages posting real-time, unfiltered and raw photos, with a 2-minute window to post each day.
      2. Wysa- An online AI therapist, modern tech used to solve real problems.
      3. Headspace - Mental health app with expert-led meditations and tools
      4. Calm- The #1 app for meditation and sleep depriviation, tackling serious problems caused by social media.
    • Habits that help you live a better life.
      1. Talk to your loved ones. Talk to your friends. Depression is a problem almost all of us face in our lives. Tackle it with love, the first step to feeling better is asking for help.
      2. Cut negativity out! Unfollow people that set unrealistic standars of living. They're 1% of the world and you see 1% of their lives. Don't let social media be the dictator of how happy you are during the day.
      3. Limit your screen time. Add locks on apps that seem unnecessary to you. Nobody needs to watch 3 hours of reels a day.
      4. Do not use your phone at least 30 minutes before bed. No screen exposure. Helps you sleep better and calm your mind.
      5. Journaling. A way to affirm you of the things you do during a day. Helps put your life into perspective. And very importantly, helps your self worth too.
